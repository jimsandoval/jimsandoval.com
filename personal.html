From Mountains to Rivers Fall 2019

Big changes in 2019. The previous blog header, preserved above, is outdated.

We've pulled up stakes in Park City and moved to Cynthia's hometown of Alexandria, Louisiana. Alexandria sits aside the Red River in the immense Mississippi river delta in what is generally called Cenla - Central Louisiana. It's primarily an agricultural area just to the north of Cajun country with sugarcane and cotton fields to the south and pine forests to the north. This move has been in the works for some time and we're excited about it. I've already succumbed to Louisiana's legendary cuisine and put on a few more pounds than I should.

Winter 2019

Tons of snow this year. I've taken up cross-country skiing - both skate and classic. Really been enjoying it. This pic is looking back into town from where the rail trail crosses the Richardson Flat Rd. I was the first one on the trail a couple of days after a big four-day storm cycle.

Moose Summer, Fall 2018

Cynthia got Ava the dog she's wanted for a while. She said "I know you're not crazy about getting a dog but we're going to get one anyway." #dadlife LOL.

Ava named him "Moose." They tell me he's an aussiedoodle. Nice dog. Instant family member. At first he looked more like a bear cub than a dog, and Ava wanted to name him "Bear" but our neighbors had just lost their dog Bear to old age, so she decided on Moose. I was pulling for Swampy or Dusty. The first couple pictures are from August, the third from October.

Moose Late Winter 2018

We had a juvenile moose hanging around the backyard for a while. I was reading in the living room on an early March Sunday afternoon and he came down from the rail trail, hung out a bit, then crossed the creek towards the house and followed the creek down. I could see him through the window, so I took this video.

Moose are not uncommon in the neighborhood, but they aren't common enough for it to be an everyday kind of thing.

Summer 2016

These shoes pretty much sum up my summer. Work, cycling, and landscaping the front yard. The work boots have earned their keep. I was a bit on the fence whether to do the landscaping myself or hiring someone to do it, but the decision was made by the several Park City landscaping companies that did not even return my phone calls. My buddy David is working on a $300,000 deck project (!) here in town, so I guess it makes sense these guys don't want to bother with a lowly 4 digit payday.

Lately, the first thing I do every morning is check how much grass seed has sprouted in the yard. Yes, I'm actually excited to watch grass grow. Ava's been getting a good laugh out of what a lame middle-aged man I've become. I can't argue with that.
